# Blessed Crow — PvP Guide

> **Faction:** Tuatha Dé Danann
> **Trees:** Class Abilities · Dominance · Nourishment · Ritual
> **Resource:** Faith + Judgment (dual converter)
> **Group Role:** Healer (main healer of this comp)
> **One-line identity:** The Cauldron field — turns the ground the group fights on into overlapping zones of healing, cleanse, damage-amp, and battle-res that no other class can replicate.

---

## Core Principles

> If you remember nothing else, remember these.

- **Cauldrons are the entire class.** Summon Cauldron, Bran's Aid, Call Down, and Blood Offering all place Cauldrons (max 4 total), and your strongest abilities — Rite of Healing, Cleansing Winds, Rite of Ruin, Rite of Sickness, Dark Work, Bran's Gift, Conduit of Power — all key off "each Cauldron." More Cauldrons on the group means more of everything good.
- **Position the field over the group, not over the enemy.** Cleansing Winds and Rite of Healing radiate from you *and* each Cauldron. If the Cauldrons aren't where your allies stand, half your kit does nothing.
- **The dual resource is a converter, not a bar.** Spending Judgment refunds large Faith; spending Faith refunds small Judgment. Keep both moving so your big heals never starve.
- **Cleanse is your trump card against the enemy's win condition.** Cleansing Winds removes a debuff near you and near each Cauldron and the text says it "can apply multiple times" — it is the single best answer to DoT/poison teams trying to out-pressure your healing.
- **Battle-res is positional.** Bran's Gift resurrects allies near each of *your* Cauldrons. Keep Cauldrons on the group so a wipe-saving res is actually possible.
- **You are not the kill threat.** You carry weapon hits and snares, but your job is the field and the triage, never the chase.

## Role in the Group

### If Healer
- **Primary job:** Keep the Fianna frontline and the dive core (Red Cap in melee, Forest Stalker on the flank) alive through their commitment windows. Priority: the Fianna or whoever is being focused first, then your co-healer the Empath, then the backline (Dark Fool, Forest Stalker), then yourself.
- **Positioning:** Heals reach 45m, but you are Cauldron-anchored. Place the Cauldron field just behind the Fianna's line where the group will hold, keeping line of sight to allies. Do not wander off your own field to chase an offensive cast.
- **Triage priority:** Single-target clutch is Branwen's Tears (2813). Sustained party healing is Regrowth (2250 AoE), Rally (party HoT), and Rite of Healing (Cauldron periodic). Cleanse is Cleansing Winds. Battle-res is Bran's Gift (multi) or Rise from the Cauldron (single). When several allies are low: AoE/Cauldron heals first if they're grouped, single-target clutch if one ally is about to die.
- **Cleanse responsibility:** You are the *area* cleanser — Cleansing Winds blankets the group and every Cauldron. Let the Empath handle single-target and Link cleanse (Leigheas, Comhinannu) so you aren't both spending on the same debuff.
- **Battle-res:** Use Bran's Gift when two or more allies are down near your Cauldrons (25% max HP). Use Rise from the Cauldron only for a single clutch res when you are safe — it is a 9.0s cast and will be interrupted under pressure. A 9s res mid-fight is usually a trap; don't start it while being hit.
- **What you do NOT do:** Don't chase kills with Crushing Might or Overwhelm. Don't push Cauldrons into the enemy backline for offense and abandon your heal field. Don't tunnel damage while the group bleeds out.

## Resource System

**Faith + Judgment (dual converter):** Judgment is a small pool (abilities cost it in single digits); Faith is a large pool (costs and refunds in the hundreds). Your big heals and auras spend Judgment and refund Faith; your utility, snares, and cleanse spend Faith and refund Judgment. You alternate between the two currencies rather than building one and dumping it.

**Generators (top up Judgment):**
- Crushing Might — +1 Judgment (weapon hit, 972 damage)
- Nature's Rebuke — +1 Judgment (weapon hit + snare, 864 damage)
- Faith-spenders that refund Judgment: Cleansing Winds (−150 Faith, +1), Nurture Life (−100 Faith, +1), Sacred Pride (−125 Faith, +3), Scorn the Weak (−150 Faith, +Judgment), Dedicated Pursuit (−125 Faith, +1), Dark Work (−150 Faith, +1)

**Spenders (dump Judgment, refund Faith):**
- Rally (−2 Judgment, +500 Faith), Regrowth (−2, +500), Rite of Healing (−2, +500), Living Symbol (−2, +500), Rite of Ruin (−3, +750), Branwen's Tears (−1, +250), Rite of Sickness (−1, +250), Overwhelm (−1, +250), Strike of the Blessed (−1, +250), Conduit of Power (−1, +250)
- Free (no resource): Summon Cauldron, Bran's Aid, Bran's Gift, Call Down, Blood Offering, Armor of Faith, Avatar of Bran

**The loop:** Weave a free weapon hit (Crushing Might / Nature's Rebuke) or a Faith-spender to top your Judgment, then dump Judgment into your big party heals, which refund a large chunk of Faith — which in turn pays for your next cleanse/snare/utility. Cardinal rules: don't let Judgment bottom out right before a heal-heavy window, and don't sit at full Faith (you waste the refund). *Inference: the database gives the per-ability deltas but not the pool sizes or caps, so treat "it sustains as a loop" as derived from the deltas, not a stated number.*

## Pre-Engage

- **Summon Cauldron / Call Down / Blood Offering** — pre-place the Cauldron field where the group will hold the fight. Forgiving and pre-stageable, but each lasts 60s, so place within roughly a minute of contact so they don't expire before the fight (inference from the 60s duration).
- **Living Symbol** — Panic-resistance + armor aura; stage it before contact if you expect a Dark Fool / Panic-heavy enemy.
- **Armor of Faith** — +70% Physical Armor for 8s. Press it last, right before contact, so the short window doesn't lapse.
- **Rite of Healing / Rite of Ruin** — prime your Cauldron auras as the fight opens (heal field on you, damage-taken field on them).
- **Conditional staging** — Avatar of Bran (120s CD) if you expect to eat a big hit early.

[Approach: ride the party's speed buffs (Dark Fool's Song of Travel, Forest Stalker's Reposition) into position rather than over-extending to place Cauldrons forward.]

## Engage / Opener

> As a healer you have one opener mode: set the field and prime the amp.

1. Confirm Cauldrons are down on the group (Summon Cauldron / Bran's Aid) — purpose: activate every "near each Cauldron" effect.
2. Rite of Healing — purpose: start the periodic Cauldron heal before damage lands.
3. Rite of Ruin — purpose: drop the stacking damage-taken aura on the enemy cluster so the kill core's opener hits harder.
4. Rally — purpose: party HoT as contact begins.

**Opener goal:** Group standing inside a heal-and-cleanse field, enemy standing inside a stacking damage-taken field, both healers topped on resource.

## Core Rotation

**Maintain:**
- Rite of Healing (Cauldron periodic) and Rally (party HoT) uptime
- Rite of Ruin stacks on the enemy kill target / cluster
- Cauldron count at or near 4 — refresh as they expire

**Loop:**
- Nurture Life and Regrowth for steady throughput; Branwen's Tears for spot heals on a focused ally
- Cleansing Winds the moment meaningful debuffs land (it's on a 6s cooldown — don't hold it)
- Weave Crushing Might / Nature's Rebuke to refill Judgment when no one needs a heal

**Burst window — Rite of Ruin stacking (the aura text says it stacks):**
- Keep stacking Rite of Ruin during the team's called kill window → pair with Overwhelm (+50% damage from that ability) if you can safely reach melee, to add to the spike.

## Burst / Execute Phase

> Your "burst" is enabling the kill and surviving the trade — not dealing it.

1. **Rite of Ruin** — keep the damage-taken aura stacked on the kill target as the team commits.
2. **Conduit of Power** — +50% per nearby Cauldron, if you're safely in range and not needed for heals.
3. Hold **Rite of Healing / Regrowth** up so your divers survive the all-in.
4. **Cleansing Winds** — strip the snares/roots the enemy throws on your assassins to keep them on the target.

## Defensive Layer / Survival

**Immediate response:**
1. Armor of Faith — +70% Physical Armor for 8s
2. Living Symbol — armor + Panic-resistance aura (also helps the group)
3. Avatar of Bran — armor + Weaponskill buff (120s CD) for a heavier hit

**Reinforce / mitigate:**
- Strike of the Blessed — Spirit damage that heals you over time
- Stand on your Cauldrons so Rite of Healing tops you passively

**If still losing the exchange:**
- Dedicated Pursuit (move speed) to reposition back onto your Cauldron field
- Call the Fianna to peel; you are not built to win a 1v1 trade

**The rule:** Your survival is positional. Getting back onto your Cauldrons is your real defensive cooldown — don't stand and trade.

## Break CC

> Be honest with the group: the database shows **no dedicated CC-break** for the Blessed Crow. Your CC plan is prevention plus your teammates.

**You are soft CC'd (rooted/snared):**
- No innate break. Reposition with Dedicated Pursuit when the snare allows, and call for peel.

**You are hard CC'd (stunned/feared):**
- No innate break. This is why you stand inside the group — so the Fianna can peel and the Empath can cover you.

**Priority rule:** Living Symbol grants Panic Resistance (your only innate CC-mitigation, and only vs Panic). Beyond that, ask the Empath to make *you* their Friendly Link so you receive Comhinannu (removes all debuffs) and Atreisiú (−10% damage taken). Positioning is your break CC.

**After breaking (when peeled free):** Get back onto the Cauldron field and resume triage immediately.

## Peel / Protecting the Backline

- **Scorn the Weak** (25m) — your ranged peel: snare + −30% damage dealt on a diver hitting your backline. Best opener against someone diving the Empath or Dark Fool.
- **Sacred Pride / Nature's Rebuke** — melee-range snare peels if a threat reaches you.
- **Rite of Sickness** — Cauldron AoE snare on anyone fighting inside your field.

**Peel priority:** Scorn the Weak the diver first (range + damage reduction does the most to blunt them) → Rite of Sickness if they're standing in your Cauldrons → hand them to the Fianna for hard lockdown, then return to healing.

## Target Priority / Matchups

You don't choose kill targets; you enable the team's call. Drop Rite of Ruin and (when reachable) Overwhelm on whatever the group focuses — per the comp plan, that's usually the enemy healer first. Against DoT/poison teams (including a mirror Red Cap or Empath), Cleansing Winds is your most important ability — keep the field tight to the group and cleanse on cooldown. Against Panic / Dark Fool teams, pre-stage Living Symbol and keep its aura up. Against dive comps, keep Cauldrons hugging the group and lean on Scorn the Weak plus Fianna peel rather than pushing the field forward for offensive Cauldron damage.

## Class-Specific Mechanics

- **Cauldrons (max 4 total).** Summon Cauldron, Bran's Aid, Call Down, and Blood Offering each place a Cauldron lasting 60s. Density equals power — keep the field on the group.
