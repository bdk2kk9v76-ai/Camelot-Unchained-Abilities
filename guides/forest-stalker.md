# Forest Stalker — PvP Guide

> **Faction:** Tuatha Dé Danann
> **Trees:** Class Abilities · Predation · Stone Rain · Thorn and Fang
> **Resource:** Focus (built by big hits, spent on finishers/buffs) + Aftershock interaction
> **Group Role:** Damage (ranged finisher / anti-heal)
> **One-line identity:** The healer-killer at range — cuts the enemy healer's healing in half, denies their escape, and delivers missing-HP execute shots from 70m while the melee can't be kited.

---

## Core Principles

> If you remember nothing else, remember these.

- **Necrotic Toxin is your most important button.** It reduces the target's healing received by 50% for 12s. On the enemy healer or the kill target, it's what lets the team's burst actually stick. Lead with it on the kill window.
- **Mark Target shuts the door.** It raises the target's damage taken *and* prevents them from Stealthing or entering the Veil for 10s — your answer to slippery assassins and your own anti-escape on the kill target.
- **Build Focus on your big hits, spend it on finishers.** Strike and Fade, Spear of Lugh, Death's Door, Necrotic Toxin, Opening Shot, and Spore Cloud *generate* Focus; Sniper's Shot, Pick Off From Herd, Pounding Shot, Vital Focus, and Poison Arrow *spend* it. (Database convention: plain numbers = generation, a minus = spend — *inference from the sign pattern*.)
- **You punish isolation.** Pick Off From Herd does +100% if the target isn't near allies; Strike and Fade scales with missing HP and Stealths you on a kill. You're the finisher on a target the team has pulled out and worn down.
- **You feed and finish the Aftershock burst.** Your Stone Rain tree is Earth damage; Spear of Lugh Fractures. Build Aftershock with the team and detonate at range.
- **Stay at range and stay unseen.** Stealth, Prey Sight, Parting Shot, and Fall Back keep you at distance. You should rarely be the one getting hit — if you are, something has gone wrong with positioning.

## Role in the Group

### If Damage
- **Primary job:** Anti-heal and ranged finishing on the called kill target — the **enemy healer** first per the comp plan. You strip their healing (Necrotic Toxin), pin their escape (Mark Target), and deliver execute-scaling shots while the Red Cap dives and the Dark Fool amps.
- **Positioning:** Backline/flank at 60–70m, behind the Fianna's line, with clear sightlines to the kill target. You don't need to dive — your range is your safety. Reposition with Parting Shot (leap back + snare) when pressured.
- **Focus fire:** Open the anti-heal and Mark on the team's call, then commit your Focus spenders (Sniper's Shot, Pick Off From Herd) into the burst window. Hold your execute shots for when the target is low or isolated.
- **Peel responsibility:** You carry useful ranged peel (Crippling Shot root, Fist of Danu knockback, Parting Shot snare, Spike Field zone) for threats reaching your healers — but your priority is killing the called target; peel only when a diver is actually on the backline.
- **What you do NOT do:** Don't abandon range to brawl. Don't waste Mark Target on a target that can't escape anyway (save it for the assassin or the healer you must pin). Don't blow Sniper's Shot / your execute shots before the anti-heal and amp are on the target.

## Resource System

**Focus (build / spend).** Unlike the Dark Fool, your Focus has clear *generators*: several of your hardest hits add Focus, and your finishers spend it. (The database lists generators as a plain number and spenders with a minus — read as build/spend; *inference from the sign convention*.)

**Generators (build Focus):**
- Strike and Fade (+300), Spear of Lugh (+350), Death's Door (+350), Necrotic Toxin (+300), Spore Cloud (+250), Opening Shot (+150)

**Spenders (dump Focus):**
- Pick Off From Herd (−175, +100% if isolated), Vital Focus (−150, next hit 100% crit), Pounding Shot (−125, uses Aftershock), Sniper's Shot (−100, 100% crit), Poison Arrow (−100)
- Free (no Focus): Mark Target, Reposition, Arrow Shot, Crippling Shot, Parting Shot, Prey Sight, Scent Blood, Stealth, Claws of Earth, Cloaked in Earth, Earthen Arrow, Fist of Danu, Spike Field, Distilled Poison, Fall Back, Needle Shot

**The loop:** Generate Focus with your heavy hits (Necrotic Toxin, Death's Door, Spear of Lugh, Opening Shot) and spend it on your guaranteed-crit and execute shots (Sniper's Shot, Pick Off From Herd, with Vital Focus to make the next one a 100% crit). Because Necrotic Toxin both anti-heals *and* builds Focus, it's the ideal opener. ## Pre-Engage

- **Stealth / Prey Sight** — open from Stealth where possible; Prey Sight also boosts your Stealth Detection (+25m) and Crit chance — useful to spot enemy assassins before they reach your healers.
- **Distilled Poison** — +15% Periodic Poison damage as a standing buff if you'll lean on poison shots.
- **Cloaked in Earth** — reduces the damage of your next 3 hits received (60s); stage if you expect to be poked on approach.
- **Reposition** — party move speed (4s) to help the group reach position.

[Approach: you don't charge — set up at range from Stealth and let the Fianna/Red Cap engage; you open the kill from distance.]

## Engage / Opener

> Two opener modes: the ranged anti-heal opener and the stealth pick.

**Mode A — Anti-heal opener (primary, on the enemy healer):**
1. Mark Target — +damage taken and prevent Stealth/Veil escape (purpose: pin the healer so they can't slip the focus).
2. Necrotic Toxin — −50% healing received for 12s (purpose: gut their self-/team-healing before the burst) and bank Focus.
3. Scent Blood — long Slashing DoT + a visible blood trail (purpose: sustained pressure + tracking if they run).

**Mode B — Stealth pick (on an isolated squishy):**
1. Opening Shot — +8% of current Health as bonus damage (purpose: big opener from full Focus-generation).
2. Pick Off From Herd — +100% if they're away from allies (purpose: punish isolation hard).
3. Strike and Fade — missing-HP execute; on a kill you re-Stealth and gain move speed (purpose: finish and reset for the next pick).

**Opener goal:** Kill target Marked (can't escape), anti-healed (−50%), and bleeding, with your Focus banked for execute shots.

## Core Rotation

**Maintain:**
- Necrotic Toxin's −50% healing refreshed on the kill target (12s window)
- Scent Blood DoT ticking (and the blood trail on a runner)
- Mark Target up on a target that's trying to disengage/stealth
- Focus banked for your spenders

**Loop:**
- Generate with Death's Door / Spear of Lugh / Opening Shot / Necrotic Toxin
- Spend on Sniper's Shot (100% crit) and Pick Off From Herd (especially on isolated targets)
- Vital Focus before a key shot to guarantee a 100% crit
- Build Aftershock with Earthen Arrow / Fist of Danu and use Pounding Shot (consumes 40% of Aftershock as bonus) / Spear of Lugh (Fracture) to cash it

**Burst window (on the team's call):**
- Vital Focus → Sniper's Shot (guaranteed crit) into the Dark Fool's amp, with Necrotic Toxin already cutting the target's healing — your highest-value spike.

## Burst / Execute Phase

> When the target is low or isolated, your shots scale up hard.

1. **Strike and Fade** — bonus damage equal to 15% of the target's missing HP, and on a killing blow you immediately Stealth + gain move speed. Primary execute as they fall.
2. **Pick Off From Herd** — +100% damage if the target isn't near allies; if the team (or your Fianna's pull) has isolated them, this is your biggest hit.
3. **Sniper's Shot** (100% crit) with **Vital Focus** pre-loaded — guaranteed crit kill-secure shot.
4. **Death's Door** — increases the target's Periodic Poison damage taken by 200% while below 30% HP for 20s; layer it if poison DoTs are on the target during the execute.

## Defensive Layer / Survival

> Your defense is distance and avoidance — disengage rather than trade.

**Immediate response:**
1. Parting Shot — leap backward, deal damage, and Snare the pursuer (4/7s): your main "get off me"
2. Whirling-style ranged avoidance: Cloaked in Earth — reduces the damage of your next 3 hits received
3. Fall Back — grants move speed (note: it also Disarms you, per the text — a mobility tool, not an offensive one)

**Reinforce / mitigate:**
- Spike Field — area around you that Snares enemies 50% and damages them; drop it to discourage a melee diver and create a kite zone

**If still losing the exchange:**
- Stealth / Fade Into Trees — break the enemy's target on you and reset (Fade Into Trees breaks on move/ability, so use it to drop combat, not to travel)
- Reposition to fresh ground behind the Fianna

**The rule:** You win by never being in melee. If someone's on you, Parting Shot to create separation + snare, drop Spike Field to slow them, and re-establish range — don't stand and brawl.

## Break CC

> Be honest with the group: the database shows **no dedicated CC-break** for the Forest Stalker. Your CC plan is distance, snares, and avoidance.

**You are soft CC'd (rooted/snared):**
- No innate cleanse. Parting Shot leaps you backward (creating distance even while snared) and snares the pursuer; Fall Back grants move speed to shake a slow. Use range so a snare matters less.

**You are hard CC'd (stunned):**
- No innate break. Avoid it by staying at 60–70m behind the Fianna; rely on the Empath's coverage and the Fianna's peel if an assassin reaches you.

**Priority rule:** Prevention over cure — Prey Sight and Mark Target help you spot and pin would-be divers *before* they reach you. Against a target that keeps closing, Crippling Shot (root) and Spike Field keep them off you.

**After breaking (when freed):** Re-establish range immediately, re-apply Mark/Necrotic Toxin on the kill target, and resume from distance.

## Peel / Protecting the Backline

> Solid ranged peel — use it when a diver is genuinely on your healers.

- **Crippling Shot** — Roots a diver in place (ranged): your cleanest peel.
- **Fist of Danu** — Earth damage + Knockback: shove a melee off the backline.
- **Parting Shot** — Snare + your own repositioning, if the diver is on *you* near the backline.
- **Spike Field** — drop it on the healers' position to Snare (50%) anyone diving in.
- **Mark Target** — on an enemy assassin, it prevents them re-Stealthing/Veiling to escape after you expose them.

**Peel priority:** Crippling Shot to root the diver → Fist of Danu to knock them off if they break root → Spike Field to zone the area. Then back to your kill target. Mark the assassin if they're the recurring threat so they can't keep vanishing.

## Target Priority / Matchups

Your job is the **enemy healer** first — Mark Target to deny escape, Necrotic Toxin to halve their healing, then execute shots as the team brings them down. After the healer, shift to isolated squishies where Pick Off From Herd (+100%) and Strike and Fade (missing-HP) pay off most. You're the comp's answer to **kiting**: at 70m you can't be out-ranged by melee, and Mark Target + Crippling Shot keep runners from escaping. Against enemy assassins diving your healers, Prey Sight (detection) + Crippling Shot + Mark Target (no re-stealth) shut them down. Hold your execute shots until anti-heal and the Dark Fool's amp are on the target so the burst isn't healed back.

## Class-Specific Mechanics

- **Stealth + anti-escape.** Stealth makes you invisible outside enemy Detection Range; Prey Sight raises your own detection +25m. Mark Target denies the *enemy's* Stealth/Veil for 10s — making you the comp's counter to stealth/Veil classes (including a mirror Red Cap or Forest Stalker).
- **Aftershock (cross-class Earth system).** Your Stone Rain tree deals Earth damage; Pounding Shot deals bonus "equal to 40% of Aftershock" and Spear of Lugh "Fractures the target." *Inference:* the text confirms Pounding Shot and Spear of Lugh interact with Aftershock, but does not explicitly state that every Earthen hit builds it — coordinate with the Red Cap (Undermine builds, Final Fracture detonates) and Fianna (Crushing Earth builds, Nature's Sundering detonates) to stack and cash Aftershock on the kill target, treating cross-class pooling as a likely-but-unstated synergy.
- **Execute / isolation scaling.** Pick Off From Herd (+100% isolated), Strike and Fade (missing-HP + stealth-on-kill), Opening Shot (+8% current Health), and Death's Door (+200% periodic poison damage taken under 30%) all reward finishing a worn-down or isolated target — you're the closer, not the opener of damage.
