# Fianna — PvP Guide

> **Faction:** Tuatha Dé Danann
> **Trees:** Class Abilities · Form of Danu · Prowess · Resilience
> **Resource:** Courage (build / spend) + Guts (secondary defensive resource) + Aftershock interaction
> **Group Role:** Tank / peel frontline
> **One-line identity:** The wall and the winch — the only class that can pull a kill target out of position *and* hold the line in front of two healers, punishing everyone who tries to walk through.

---

## Core Principles

> If you remember nothing else, remember these.

- **Your pulls and roots start the kill.** Inescapable Vines (pull + root) and Roots of the Earth (charge + AoE root) yank the enemy healer or a fleeing target into your team's burst. Landing these is your single biggest contribution.
- **You stand between the enemy and your healers.** Whirlwind knocks back *everything except* your group; Natural Strength knocks back and stuns a cleave; Rebuke Foe and Bash add more. You are the body that makes diving the Blessed Crow and Empath miserable.
- **Build Courage on cheap strikes, spend it on the big ones.** Earthen Strike, Rending Strike, and Forest Strike feed Courage; Whirlwind, Cry of the Land, and Crushing Earth spend it.
- **Cry of the Land refunds if it kills.** Save your 200-Courage finisher for a target that will actually die to it — a kill makes it free.
- **You punish attackers passively.** Earth's Gift, Forest of Thorns, Stony Reprisal, and Nature's Defense reflect or retaliate. The longer the enemy hits you, the more it costs them — lean into being focused.
- **You are self-sufficient on CC and sustain.** Unstoppable River clears your own control, Cry of the Forest heals and frees you from roots/snares, and Restore Vitae tops you between trades. Don't rely on the healers to babysit you.

## Role in the Group

### If Tank
- **Primary job:** Peel for the backline (Blessed Crow, Empath, Dark Fool) and control space so the enemy can't freely reach them, while setting up kills by yanking priority targets into your team. You hold the front; you also winch the enemy healer out of safety.
- **Positioning:** Front line, between the enemy and your two healers. Hold the choke / the ground your Cauldrons are on so the Blessed Crow's field stays useful. Body-block lanes to the backline rather than chasing into the enemy.
- **Peel toolkit:** Inescapable Vines (pull + root 3/5s) and Roots of the Earth (charge + AoE root 4/8s) for lockdown; Whirlwind (AoE knockback that spares allies) and Natural Strength (cleave knockback + stun 2/4s) to clear a dogpile off your healers; Rebuke Foe (knockback) and Bash (stun) as single-target interrupts/peels.
- **Threat denial:** Earth's Gift (+DR, reflects Ranged), Forest of Thorns (reflect 50%), Stony Reprisal (next block reflects 50% as Earth), and Nature's Defense (+55% Parry, retaliate) make you painful to attack — plus your higher health regen lets you hold longer. Be the target they can't profitably ignore.
- **What you do NOT do:** Don't chase a kill away from your group and leave the healers exposed. Don't waste Inescapable Vines / Roots of the Earth on a low-value target — they're for the enemy healer or the player the team is killing. Don't blow Cry of the Land as a filler when it could be a refunded finisher.

## Resource System

**Courage (build / spend), plus Guts and Aftershock.** Courage is a pool you fill with light strikes and dump on heavy hits and Whirlwind. Guts is a secondary defensive resource your buffs scale. Your Earth attacks also interact with the cross-class Aftershock system.

**Generators (build Courage):**
- Earthen Strike (+75 Courage), Forest Strike (+75), Rending Strike (+50), Restore Vitae (+100, also self-heal), Trust in the Forest (+600 Courage based on missing Health — your big refill when low)

**Spenders (dump Courage):**
- Whirlwind (−300, AoE knockback), Cry of the Land (−200, *refunded on kill*), Crushing Earth (−125, +Aftershock), Nature's Vengeance (−125, execute), Nature's Cry (−100), Strike of Wolves (−75)
- Free (no Courage): Bash, Shield Wall, Living Stone, Nature's Sundering, Stony Reprisal, Inescapable Vines, Natural Strength, Rebuke Foe, Unstoppable River, Cry of the Forest, Nature's Defense, Oakenshield, Roots of the Earth, Earth's Gift, Forest of Thorns

**The loop:** Open and fill with Earthen Strike / Rending Strike / Forest Strike, then spend on Whirlwind (to peel) or your damage finishers. When you drop low, Trust in the Forest converts missing Health into a +600 Courage dump (and buffs Guts). Cardinal rules: don't sit at full Courage (you're losing generation), and hold Cry of the Land for a target it can finish so the cost refunds. *Inference: starting pool, cap, and what Guts actually does mechanically are not defined in the database — treat Guts as a secondary defensive value your buffs raise, function unspecified.*

## Pre-Engage

- **Shield Wall** — significantly increases block chance and damage reduction; press right before contact when you'll be the one absorbing the opener.
- **Earth's Gift** — +5% Physical / +10% Magic DR and reflects Ranged for 10s; stage just before contact, especially vs ranged pressure.
- **Forest of Thorns** — +30% Guts and turns your passive into a 50% reflect for 10s; stage if you expect to be focused.
- **Trust in the Forest** — if you took chip damage on approach, convert missing Health to Courage and buff Guts before engaging.

[Approach: charge in via Inescapable Vines / Roots of the Earth / Unstoppable River, or ride the party's speed buffs to position on the front line.]

## Engage / Opener

> Two opener modes depending on whether you're starting the kill or holding the line.

**Mode A — Start the kill (winch a target in):**
1. Inescapable Vines — pull the enemy healer/kill target to you and root them (purpose: deliver them into your team's burst).
2. Bash — stun to extend the lockdown while the Red Cap / Forest Stalker open.
3. Crushing Earth — Earth damage that adds Aftershock for the team's Fracture (purpose: feed the Aftershock burst).

**Mode B — Hold the line (peel-first):**
1. Roots of the Earth — charge in and AoE-root the enemy front so they can't reach your healers.
2. Whirlwind — knock the dogpile back (spares your allies).
3. Shield Wall — settle in as the anchor and soak.

**Opener goal:** Either the kill target is pulled, rooted, and locked for the team's spike — or the enemy front is rooted/knocked off your backline and you're the immovable anchor.

## Core Rotation

**Maintain:**
- Block/parry mitigation up (Shield Wall, Nature's Defense, Oakenshield as windows allow)
- Reflect/retaliate uptime (Earth's Gift / Forest of Thorns / Stony Reprisal) while being attacked
- Courage in a usable band — build with strikes, don't cap

**Loop:**
- Build with Earthen Strike / Rending Strike / Forest Strike
- Spend on Nature's Cry / Strike of Wolves for pressure, or hold Courage for Whirlwind when a peel is needed
- Weave Crushing Earth to lay Aftershock on the kill target for the team's Fracture
- Restore Vitae between trades for self-sustain

**Peel window (~CDs):**
- Inescapable Vines (45s) → Roots of the Earth (90s) → Whirlwind / Natural Strength — chain your control so there's always an answer when a threat reaches the backline.

## Burst / Execute Phase

> When the target drops low, you pivot from holding to finishing.

1. **Nature's Vengeance** — Weapon damage increased by the target's percent missing Health; this is your execute, becomes primary as they fall.
2. Keep **Crushing Earth** feeding Aftershock so the Red Cap's Final Fracture / your Nature's Sundering detonate for more.
3. **Cry of the Land** — your kill-secure: if it lands the killing blow, the 200 Courage refunds. Press it when the target will die to it.
4. **Nature's Sundering** — Fractures the target for damage equal to their Aftershock (60s CD); use it to cash in the Aftershock the team has stacked.

## Defensive Layer / Survival

**Immediate response (fire together):**
1. Shield Wall — block chance + damage reduction
2. Nature's Defense — +55% Parry for 6s and Earth retaliation on attackers
3. Cry of the Forest — heals 4746, removes Root/Snare, and grants Root/Snare immunity for 6s

**Reinforce / mitigate:**
- Oakenshield — next block's value +2000 (stack it with Shield Wall so the block you actually take is huge)
- Stony Reprisal — next block within 5s reflects 50% as Earth (pairs with Oakenshield/Nature's Defense to punish the attacker on the same block)

**If still losing the exchange:**
- Trust in the Forest — convert missing Health into Courage (+600) and Guts to fuel a comeback
- Restore Vitae — 1800 self-heal over 18s
- Unstoppable River — remove all control from yourself and charge a target to disengage or re-peel

**The rule:** Stack your block buffs on the *same* block — Oakenshield (+2000 value) + Stony Reprisal (reflect) + Nature's Defense (parry/retaliate) all want to land together so one big incoming hit becomes a big punish. Don't stagger them across separate small hits.

## Break CC

> You are one of the comp's two self-sufficient CC answers — protect the separation.

**You are soft CC'd (rooted/snared):**
- Cry of the Forest — heals you, removes Root and Snare, and grants Root/Snare immunity for 6s. Press this for movement-CC.
- Backup: Unstoppable River also removes all control from you (and charges a target) if Cry of the Forest is down.

**You are hard CC'd (stunned/knocked):**
- Unstoppable River — removes *all* control effects from you. Save it for hard CC; don't burn it on a snare you could have walked out of with Cry of the Forest.

**Priority rule:** Cry of the Forest answers Root/Snare (and heals you doing it); Unstoppable River answers everything but is your one full-clear — keep it for the stun/knock that's actually stopping you from peeling. Don't cross-use them.

**After breaking:** Use the freed window to re-peel — Inescapable Vines/Roots back onto the threat, or Whirlwind the pile off your healers, then reset on the front line.

## Peel / Protecting the Backline

> This is your core job, not a side duty.

- **Inescapable Vines** — pull + root (3/5s): lock a single diver in place, or yank the enemy healer back into your team.
- **Roots of the Earth** — charge + AoE root (4/8s): the answer when *multiple* threats reach the backline.
- **Whirlwind** — AoE knockback that spares your allies: push a dogpile off the healers instantly.
- **Natural Strength** — cleave knockback + stun (2/4s): clear and stun a clustered dive.
- **Rebuke Foe** (knockback) / **Bash** (stun) — single-target interrupts when one threat slips through.

**Peel priority:** For one diver, Inescapable Vines (root them in place) or Rebuke Foe (shove them out). For a group, Whirlwind / Natural Strength to clear, then Roots of the Earth to pin. Escalate to Bash only when you need a hard stop. Disengage back to the front line once the threat is handled.

---

## Target Priority / Matchups

Your control goes where the team's kill goes — Inescapable Vines / Roots of the Earth on the **enemy healer** to drag them out of their backline for the focus, per the comp plan. Your peel goes on whoever dives your healers — answer the most dangerous diver first (an enemy assassin reaching the Blessed Crow). Against kite/ranged comps, lean on Earth's Gift (reflect Ranged) and use Roots of the Earth/Inescapable Vines to close gaps the melee can't. Against dive comps, sit on the backline and recycle your CC chain. Against another tanky frontline, don't waste your pulls on *their* tank — save them for the squishy target the team is actually killing.

## Class-Specific Mechanics

- **Aftershock (cross-class Earth system).** Several of your Earth abilities reference Aftershock: Crushing Earth is tagged "+25% Aftershock" and Nature's Sundering "Fractures the target, dealing damage equal to their Aftershock and removing it." *Inference:* the text only explicitly says Crushing Earth adds Aftershock and that Nature's Sundering consumes it — it does **not** state that every Earth hit builds Aftershock, so treat Crushing Earth as your confirmed builder and don't assume the others stack it. Your role in the team's Aftershock burst is to build with Crushing Earth and detonate with Nature's Sundering, alongside the Red Cap (Undermine builds, Final Fracture detonates) and Forest Stalker.
- **Guts.** Forest of Thorns (+30% Guts) and Trust in the Forest (+10% Guts Threshold, +20% Guts Value) raise it; the database does not define what Guts does mechanically. Treat it as a secondary defensive resource that your buffs improve (function unspecified in the data).
- **Retaliation / reflects.** Earth's Gift (reflect Ranged), Forest of Thorns (reflect 50%), Stony Reprisal (block reflects 50% as Earth), Nature's Defense (Earth retaliation) — passive punishment that rewards being focused.
