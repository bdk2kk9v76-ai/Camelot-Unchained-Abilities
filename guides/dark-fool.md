# Dark Fool — PvP Guide

> **Faction:** Tuatha Dé Danann
> **Trees:** Class Abilities · Dark Fables · Mummery · Night Terrors
> **Resource:** Focus (spent on damage/AoE; most amps and control cost none)
> **Group Role:** Caster (force-multiplier / controller — the comp's keystone)
> **One-line identity:** The amplifier — makes every teammate's damage land harder, shuts the enemy healer's casts off, and pins the kill target so a two-healer comp can still delete a target.

---

## Core Principles

> If you remember nothing else, remember these.

- **You are why the kill happens.** And Our Hero Falls! (+60% damage taken under 50% HP), Death by a Thousand Cuts (party-wide stacking +up to 24% damage taken), and A Grand Tragedy (+20% party damage vs controlled targets) turn the comp's modest damage core into a lethal one. Land these on the kill window, not randomly.
- **Silence the enemy healer.** The Darkest Hour silences *and* disarms (4/8s). Timing it onto the enemy healer during the team's spike is often the whole teamfight.
- **Most of your best tools are free.** And Our Hero Falls!, The Tragic Betrayal, The Darkest Hour, Death by a Thousand Cuts, A Grand Tragedy, Bean Sidhe's Wail, Dark Allure, With Claws That Tear, and your party buffs all cost no Focus — so you can run the amp/control engine continuously and spend Focus only on direct/AoE damage.
- **Control feeds your own amp.** A Grand Tragedy gives the party +20% vs *controlled* targets — so your stuns/fears/charm (and the Fianna's roots, the Red Cap's stun) aren't just CC, they're a damage multiplier. Chain control into the burst.
- **You harden the group against ranged/magic.** That Stalk the Night (−40% non-melee damage taken) and Like Flitting Shadows (negate the next ranged hit, party-wide) are big team mitigation cooldowns — use them into enemy caster/archer pressure.
- **You are fragile and cast-locked.** Your key tools have 1–3s casts and you have no listed escape. Stand behind the Fianna, and let the team peel for you — a dead Dark Fool removes the comp's ceiling.

## Role in the Group

### If Caster
- **Primary job:** Ranged damage amplification and control. Stack damage-taken debuffs on the kill target, silence/disarm the enemy healer, and pin the target with hard CC so the Red Cap and Forest Stalker burst lands. Your output is mostly *other people's* output multiplied.
- **Positioning:** Back line, behind the Fianna, within your 45–90m ranges. Keep line of sight to the kill target for And Our Hero Falls! / The Darkest Hour, and to your party for the aura buffs. Protect your cast time — these are long casts.
- **Cast management:** Your amps and control are slow (And Our Hero Falls! 1.0s, The Darkest Hour 2.0s, A Grand Tragedy 3.0s, Dark Allure 7.0s channel). Hard-cast them from safety; if you're being dived, fall back to your instant/short tools (A Glimpse of Madness 0.2s Panic, A Sudden Fright 0.2s Fear) and call for peel rather than tunneling a 3s cast.
- **Control application:** You are the comp's silence/disarm and Panic engine. The Darkest Hour locks the enemy healer; The Tragic Betrayal (stun 3/6s), Bean Sidhe's Wail / A Sudden Fright (fear), and Dark Allure (charm) pin or remove threats; A Glimpse of Madness and With Claws That Tear apply Panic that your own kit punishes.
- **What you do NOT do:** Don't get caught in melee. Don't tunnel a long cast while being dived. Don't waste And Our Hero Falls! (90s) or The Darkest Hour (60s) early — they're kill-window tools. Don't fear a target the melee is mid-burst on if it'll send them running out of range (coordinate fears with the team).

## Resource System

**Focus (spend-only in the database).** Focus is consumed by your direct-damage and AoE abilities; the database shows **no Focus generator**, so how it regenerates over time isn't specified (treat that as a data gap, not a claim that it doesn't regen). Critically, your highest-impact abilities — the amps, the silence, the control, the party buffs — cost **no Focus at all**, so your "engine" runs free and Focus is really just your damage budget.

- Amps/debuffs: And Our Hero Falls!, Death by a Thousand Cuts, A Grand Tragedy, And (control) The Tragic Betrayal, The Darkest Hour, Bean Sidhe's Wail, Dark Allure, A Sudden Fright

Cardinal rule: because the engine is free, never skip the amps to save Focus — Focus only limits how much *personal* damage you add on top. *Inference: with no listed generator, I can't state the regen rate; budget Focus conservatively for your AoE/DoT until confirmed in-game.*

## Pre-Engage

- **That Stalk the Night** — party −40% non-melee damage taken + move speed; stage before contact, especially vs ranged/caster enemies.
- **As the Willow in a Storm** — +25% party Block Value, +10% Parry; pre-stage if the enemy is melee-burst.
- **Dance Through Flames** — +15 party Elemental resistance; stage vs elemental damage (3.0s cast — do it before contact).
- **With Claws That Tear** — make the party's Direct damage also apply Panic; stage it so the whole team's opener feeds Panic.
- **Song of Travel** — party move speed for the approach.

[These are long casts (3.0s on several) — all pre-stageable; get them up before the fight, not during it.]

## Engage / Opener

> Two opener emphases depending on whether you're amping a called kill or controlling a dive.

**Mode A — Amp the called kill (primary):**
1. The Darkest Hour — silence + disarm the enemy healer (purpose: deny their saves during the spike).
2. And Our Hero Falls! — set up +60% damage taken once the target dips under 50% (purpose: the execute multiplier).
3. Death by a Thousand Cuts — party stacking damage-taken debuff begins building on the target.

**Mode B — Control a threat / set the controlled-target bonus:**
1. The Tragic Betrayal — stun (3/6s) the kill target or a diver.
2. A Grand Tragedy — now the party deals +20% to that controlled target.
3. Cutting Whispers / Dark Comedy — Dark Comedy does +50% to Stun/Root/Fear targets, so it slots right onto your own CC.

**Opener goal:** Enemy healer silenced/disarmed, kill target stacking damage-taken debuffs and (when low) carrying And Our Hero Falls!, with control + A Grand Tragedy turning the whole team's damage up.

## Core Rotation

**Maintain:**
- Death by a Thousand Cuts stacks building on the kill target (party direct damage feeds it, up to 24%)
- That Stalk the Night uptime vs non-melee enemies
- Tales of Woe / A Cautionary Tale DoTs ticking on the target

**Loop:**
- Cycle your damage casts — Cutting Whispers, Dark Comedy (+50% vs Stun/Root/Fear), Screaming Shot, A Sharpened Tongue (+50% vs Panicked) — favoring the bonus conditions your team's CC sets up
- Re-apply control as cooldowns allow (A Glimpse of Madness Panic on 4s, fears/stuns on longer CDs)
- Keep A Grand Tragedy's +20%-vs-controlled value live whenever the target is under CC

**Burst window (And Our Hero Falls! 90s / The Darkest Hour 60s):**
- The Darkest Hour (silence enemy healer) → And Our Hero Falls! (as target passes 50%) → Look Upon Oblivion (3000 Mind over 12s) + your DoTs — stack the multipliers onto the team's spike.

## Burst / Execute Phase

> When the target is low, you become an execute multiplier.

1. **And Our Hero Falls!** — +60% damage taken while the target is under 50% HP; this is your primary execute tool — time it to the team's commit.
2. Keep **Death by a Thousand Cuts** stacked and **A Grand Tragedy** active (if the target is controlled) so every hit is amplified.
3. **Dark Comedy** — +50% damage to targets suffering Stun/Root/Fear; fire it while your CC (or the Fianna's) is on the target.
4. **The Darkest Hour** — if the enemy healer tries to save the target, silence/disarm them again to seal the kill.

## Defensive Layer / Survival

> You are fragile with no listed escape — your defense is the team plus mitigation buffs.

**Immediate response:**
1. That Stalk the Night — party −40% non-melee damage taken (helps you if the pressure is ranged/magic)
2. Like Flitting Shadows — negates the next ranged hit for each party member (great vs archers/casters diving you)
3. As the Willow in a Storm — party block/parry up

**Reinforce / mitigate:**
- Dance Through Flames — +15 Elemental resist if the threat is elemental
- Reposition to break line of sight on enemy casters

**If still losing the exchange:**
- A Glimpse of Madness (instant Panic) or A Sudden Fright (instant Fear) on the diver to buy a step
- Call the Fianna to peel — you have no self-escape in the data, so create distance with CC and let the wall handle it

**The rule:** Your survival is prevention and peel, not a panic button. Pre-stage your mitigation buffs and stay behind the Fianna; when dived, use *instant* CC (A Glimpse of Madness / A Sudden Fright), never a 2–3s cast.

## Break CC

> Be straight with the group: the database shows **no CC-break** for the Dark Fool.

**You are soft CC'd (rooted/snared):**
- No innate break. You can still cast — keep amping/controlling from where you're stuck, and reposition when it ends.

**You are hard CC'd (stunned/feared):**
- No innate break. This is your worst case; it's why positioning and peel matter so much.

**Priority rule:** You depend on the Empath for CC relief — ask to be considered for What Must Be Done coverage timing, and stand where the Fianna can peel before CC lands. Avoid getting isolated; against heavy-CC enemies, hold your engage until the Fianna and healers are with you.

**After breaking (when freed by the team):** Immediately resume the amp engine — re-apply And Our Hero Falls! / Death by a Thousand Cuts on the kill target; the fight likely paused while you were locked.

## Peel / Protecting the Backline

> You don't have knockbacks, but you have the comp's best ranged CC for peeling.

- **A Glimpse of Madness** (0.2s, Panic, 4s CD) — instant, repeatable peel on a diver.
- **A Sudden Fright** (0.2s, Fear 2/5s) — instant fear to send a diver away.
- **Bean Sidhe's Wail** (Fear in a 25m cone) — peel multiple threats clustered on the backline.
- **The Tragic Betrayal** (Stun 3/6s) / **Dark Allure** (Charm) — hard-remove a single dangerous diver (Charm turns them on their own team for up to 6s).
- **The Cruelest Joke** (AoE snare + DoT; if the target dies, nearby allies are Feared) — zone a dive and chain fear off a kill.

**Peel priority:** Instant first — A Glimpse of Madness or A Sudden Fright to immediately interrupt the dive — then escalate to The Tragic Betrayal / Dark Allure on the most dangerous single threat, or Bean Sidhe's Wail if several are stacked. Coordinate fears with the team so you don't fear a target out of your own melee's burst.

## Target Priority / Matchups

Your amps and silence go on the comp's called target — usually the **enemy healer** first (The Darkest Hour to silence/disarm, Death by a Thousand Cuts + And Our Hero Falls! to break them), then their squishiest caster/damage. Always pair A Grand Tragedy with whatever the team is hard-CCing for the +20%. Against caster/archer comps, lead with That Stalk the Night and Like Flitting Shadows to blunt their damage. Against dive comps, hold your long casts and lead with instant Panic/Fear to peel. Against heavy-Panic enemies (a mirror Dark Fool), note your team has the Blessed Crow's Living Symbol (Panic resist) and the Empath's Clear Mind — coordinate so your own Panic application still lands where it matters.

## Class-Specific Mechanics

- **Panic.** A Glimpse of Madness (instant Panic), With Claws That Tear (party Direct damage applies +50 Panic), and A Sharpened Tongue (+50% damage vs Panicked) form a Panic loop: stack Panic on the enemy, then punish it. *Inference:* the database doesn't state Panic's exact threshold or effect, only that you apply it and deal bonus damage to Panicked/controlled targets — build it as a debuff to amplify, function-detail unspecified.
- **Control-synergy amplification.** A Grand Tragedy (+20% vs controlled) and Dark Comedy (+50% vs Stun/Root/Fear targets) reward chaining your damage onto controlled enemies — your CC is a damage multiplier, not just utility.
- **Party-wide auras.** Several abilities buff the whole party (That Stalk the Night, Like Flitting Shadows, As the Willow in a Storm, Dance Through Flames, With Claws That Tear, Raise Morale, Song of Travel) — you are a mobile aura platform as much as a damage dealer.
