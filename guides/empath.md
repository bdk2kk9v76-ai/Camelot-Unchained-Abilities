# Empath — PvP Guide

> **Faction:** Tuatha Dé Danann
> **Trees:** Class Abilities · Tend the Body · Tend the Enemy · Tend the Spirit
> **Resource:** Faith + Judgment (dual converter) + Health (spent on the biggest spells)
> **Group Role:** Healer / debuffer (support healer of this comp)
> **One-line identity:** The engagement master — a Link-amplified healer who also carries the comp's emergency buttons (party −50% damage, mass CC-immunity, all-debuff cleanse) and an offensive enemy-Link that strips healing off the kill target.

---

## Core Principles

> If you remember nothing else, remember these.

- **Your Links are force multipliers — set them before the fight.** Nasc Cara (ally Link) grants them armor and makes Atreisiú, Comhroinn Cinniuint, Comhinannu, and the +50%/+40% "if Linked" healing bonuses fire. Nasc Namhaid (enemy Link) reduces their Strength/Attunement and unlocks bonus damage on your enemy-link spells.
- **You hold the panic buttons; spend them on purpose.** Seastan Deiridh (party −50% damage taken, 8s) and What Must Be Done (break all CC + 10s immunity) are 120s cooldowns. They win or lose teamfights — never burn them reactively on chip damage.
- **Comhinannu is your hard cleanse.** It removes *all* debuffs from your Friendly Link and heals you both. Link your most-focused ally and this counters enemy DoT/poison stacking in one cast.
- **Health is your fourth resource.** Comhroinn Fola spends 1000 Health, and Tonnadoir / Reabadh spend Health per second. You trade your own bar for outsized output — but only when you have the cushion.
- **You out-heal *and* anti-heal.** Comhlacht Mallacht (−25% healing received if Linked) on the enemy healer makes the team's focus-fire land for keeps. You contribute to the kill, not just the save.
- **You are second on the heal team, first on utility.** Let the Blessed Crow run the area heal/cleanse field; you cover single-target clutch, Links, and the big cooldowns.

## Role in the Group

### If Healer
- **Primary job:** Clutch single-target healing and emergency mitigation for the dive core and frontline. Priority: keep your Friendly Link (put it on the Fianna or whoever is being focused) topped, then the Blessed Crow (protect the Cauldron engine), then the backline, then yourself.
- **Positioning:** Heals reach 45–90m. Stand near the Blessed Crow but with enough separation that one enemy AoE/cast can't catch both healers. Keep line of sight to your Link and the frontline.
- **Triage priority:** Spot clutch is Uacht Danu (2936) and Saol Deontas (1900–2430). Sustained is Ciorcal na Beatha (party 2400 over 12s) and Slainte (1800 HoT, +50% if the target is your Link). For an ally about to die, Comhroinn Fola (3645, spends 1000 Health, +40% if Linked) is your biggest single heal. Order of operations: pre-empt the focus target with HoTs, hard-cast the big heal when they spike, and hold Seastan Deiridh for the enemy's burst window.
- **Cleanse responsibility:** You are the *single-target / Link* cleanser. Leigheas removes one debuff from any ally; Comhinannu removes *all* debuffs from your Friendly Link. Coordinate with the Blessed Crow so you cover the focused individual while their Cleansing Winds blankets the group.
- **Battle-res:** Athchoirigh (25% max HP, 9.0s cast) — only when safe and ideally behind the Fianna's peel. Under fire, let the Blessed Crow's Cauldron-anchored Bran's Gift handle multi-res while you keep the living alive.
- **What you do NOT do:** Don't dump Health into Comhroinn Fola when you're the one being dived. Don't pre-blow Seastan Deiridh or What Must Be Done. Don't chase enemy-Link damage when the group needs heals — your offense is a bonus layered on top of healing, not a replacement for it.

## Resource System

**Faith + Judgment (dual converter), plus Health.** Like the Blessed Crow, Judgment is a small pool and Faith is large; your big heals and Link spells cost Judgment and refund Faith, while your mid heals, DoTs, and Links cost Faith and refund small Judgment. On top of that, your heaviest single-target output spends your own **Health**.

**Generators (top up Judgment) — Faith-spenders that refund +1 Judgment:**
- Leigheas (−75 Faith), Saol Deontas (−100 Faith), Slainte (−100 Faith), Airde Friotaiocht (−250 Faith), Comhlacht Mallacht (−100 Faith), Fuil Truaillithe (−75 Faith), Comhroinn Cinniuint (−200 Faith), Comhroinn Fola (−75 Faith)

**Spenders (dump Judgment, refund Faith):**
- Ciorcal na Beatha (−2 Judgment, +500 Faith), Athghin (−2, +500), Atreoru (−2, +500), Atreisiú (−2, +500), Nochtadh (−3, +750), Uacht Danu (−1, +250)

**Faith-only / Health spends:**
- Anam Truaillithe (−175 Faith), Nasc Namhaid (−25 Faith), Nasc Cara (−25 Faith), Athchoirigh (−225 Faith)
- Health spends: Comhroinn Fola (−1000 Health, big heal), Tonnadoir (−150 Health/sec channel), Reabadh (−150 Health/sec channel)
- Free: Seastan Deiridh, What Must Be Done, Comhinannu, Balla Eiliminte, Clear Mind (−100 Panic)

**The loop:** Keep Judgment topped with your steady Faith-spending heals (Slainte, Saol Deontas, Leigheas), then dump Judgment into the big party heal (Ciorcal na Beatha) and Link buffs (Atreisiú), which refund Faith. When an ally is about to die and your own Health is healthy, convert Health into a huge heal via Comhroinn Fola. Cardinal rules: don't channel Tonnadoir/Reabadh (Health-per-second) while you're a focus target; don't let Judgment empty right before you need Ciorcal na Beatha. *Inference: pool sizes/caps aren't in the database; the converter behavior is read from the deltas.*

## Pre-Engage

- **Nasc Cara** — Link your Fianna (or primary focus target) before contact; the armor buff and "if Linked" heal bonuses make every later heal on them stronger. Forgiving — set it early.
- **Nasc Namhaid** — Link the enemy kill target (the enemy healer per the plan) to start reducing their Strength/Attunement; pre-stage if you can reach them.
- **Balla Eiliminte** — grant your Link Fire/Lightning immunity + resist if you expect elemental burst; stage before contact.
- **Airde Friotaiocht** — +60 Physical/Magical Armor on the frontline (10s) — press right before contact so the window lands during the opener.

[Approach: ride the party speed buffs in; you have no charge, so stay behind the Fianna.]

## Engage / Opener

> One opener mode: lock your Links, prime mitigation, start anti-heal.

1. Nasc Cara on the focus target / Fianna — purpose: turn on Link-amplified healing.
2. Nasc Namhaid on the enemy kill target — purpose: start the Strength/Attunement reduction and unlock your enemy-link damage.
3. Slainte + Comhroinn Cinniuint on your Link — purpose: pre-load HoTs (Slainte is +50% on a Linked target).
4. Comhlacht Mallacht on the enemy healer — purpose: −25% healing received before the team commits the kill.

**Opener goal:** Friendly Link buffed and pre-healed, enemy healer Linked and anti-healed, your two big cooldowns held in reserve.

## Core Rotation

**Maintain:**
- Slainte / Comhroinn Cinniuint HoT uptime on your Friendly Link
- Ciorcal na Beatha (party heal over 12s) on cooldown when the group is taking spread damage
- Comhlacht Mallacht refreshed on the enemy healer (−25% healing, 4s window)

**Loop:**
- Uacht Danu and Saol Deontas as your spot heals on whoever spikes low
- Atreisiú on your Link when they drop under 50% (it doubles in effect there)
- Weave Anam Truaillithe / Fuil Truaillithe DoTs onto the kill target when no one needs a heal — free pressure that helps the focus

**Burst window — enable the kill:**
- Nasc Namhaid + Comhlacht Mallacht on the enemy healer → Pairilis (disarm, +50 damage if Linked) to deny their casts as the team bursts.

---

## Burst / Execute Phase

> Your execute is anti-heal + denial so the team's spike sticks.

1. **Comhlacht Mallacht** — −25% healing received on the enemy healer (refresh through the kill window).
2. Maintain **Nasc Namhaid** on the target so your enemy-link damage and stat reduction stay active.
3. **Pairilis** — Disarms the target (3/6s), with +50 damage if they're Linked to you — shut the enemy healer's weapon/cast options during the spike.
4. **Seastan Deiridh** — if the enemy answers your all-in with their own burst, drop the party −50% damage taken to win the trade.

## Defensive Layer / Survival

**Immediate response:**
1. What Must Be Done — breaks all CC and grants 10s CC immunity (your panic button when chained)
2. Seastan Deiridh — party −50% damage taken for 8s (covers you and everyone)
3. Athghin — heals you for 4500 over 6s

**Reinforce / mitigate:**
- Comhinannu (if you're the one debuffed and you have a Link) removes all debuffs from your Link and heals you both — note it cleanses the *Link*, so the real defensive use is keeping your Link clean; for self-sustain lean on Athghin
- Airde Friotaiocht on yourself for armor

**If still losing the exchange:**
- Nochtadh — Spirit damage that heals you for 40% of your missing Health (a power spike when low)
- Retreat behind the Fianna and call for peel

**The rule:** What Must Be Done and Seastan Deiridh fire on *intent*, not reflex. If you're chained, What Must Be Done first to act; if the whole team is being bursted, Seastan Deiridh. Don't waste either on a single snare.

## Break CC

> You have the comp's best CC answer — keep it for the moment that matters.

**You are soft CC'd (rooted/snared):**
- What Must Be Done breaks it, but it's a 120s cooldown — only use it on soft CC if the snare is what's getting you killed. Otherwise heal through it; you can cast while rooted.

**You are hard CC'd (stunned/feared/chained):**
- What Must Be Done — breaks all CC and grants 10s immunity. This is what it's for. Save it for a hard chain on you or a moment the group must act through CC.

**Priority rule:** What Must Be Done is self-only and on a long cooldown — it answers hard chains, not chip CC. For Panic specifically, Clear Mind (−350 Panic) is a cheaper, repeatable tool you can use on allies or yourself-adjacent targets.

**After breaking:** Use the 10s immunity window to stabilize the group — land Ciorcal na Beatha / Comhroinn Fola uninterrupted, then reset.

---

## Peel / Protecting the Backline

> Your peel is denial and mitigation, not knockbacks.

- **Pairilis** — Disarm (3/6s, +50 damage if Linked) on a diver hitting your backline; shuts off their weapon damage.
- **Nasc Namhaid** — Link a diver to cut their Strength/Attunement, weakening their output while they're on you.
- **Seastan Deiridh** — if multiple threats break through, the party −50% buys the whole backline time.

**Peel priority:** Pairilis the diver first (disarm denies the most damage) → Nasc Namhaid to weaken them → call the Fianna for hard lockdown. Disengage to healing as soon as the Fianna has them.

---

## Target Priority / Matchups

Per the comp plan, your enemy Link goes on the **enemy healer**: Nasc Namhaid to weaken them, Comhlacht Mallacht to cut their healing, Pairilis to disarm them during the spike. Your Friendly Link goes on the **most-focused ally** — usually the Fianna up front or whichever diver the enemy trains. Against DoT/poison teams, Comhinannu (all-debuff cleanse on your Link) is your standout; pre-Link the player most likely to be loaded with DoTs. Against burst/CC comps, hold Seastan Deiridh and What Must Be Done for their commit. Avoid spending Health (Comhroinn Fola, channels) while you're the focus target.

## Class-Specific Mechanics

- **Links (ally + enemy).** Nasc Cara Links an ally (grants them armor) and Nasc Namhaid Links an enemy (reduces Strength/Attunement). Many abilities scale "if the target is Linked": Slainte (+50% healing), Comhroinn Fola (+40% healing), Pairilis (+50 damage), and your Friendly-Link-only spells (Atreisiú, Comhroinn Cinniuint, Comhinannu, Balla Eiliminte). Keeping both Links live is most of your power.
- **Health as fuel.** Comhroinn Fola spends 1000 Health for a 3645 heal; Tonnadoir and Reabadh channel at 150 Health/sec. Nochtadh and Comhinannu convert your missing Health into healing for others (40% of missing Health). You are designed to run your own bar lower than a normal healer would — manage the cushion.
- *Inference flag:* whether two stacks of Comhlacht Mallacht (or your −25% plus the Forest Stalker's −50% heal debuff) stack additively is not stated in the database; assume they layer as separate debuffs but confirm in-game.
